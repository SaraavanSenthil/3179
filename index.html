Olympic Medal Tally 2004-2020

The medal tally highlights the dominance of certain nations in the Summer Olympics over the given period. Notably, the United States leads the tally with a remarkable total of 1390 medals, out of which 641 are Gold, 406 are Silver, and 343 are Bronze. Russia and Germany secure the subsequent positions with total medals of 735 and 579 respectively. China is not far behind with a total of 657 medals, displaying a strong competition among these nations.

In the subsequent ranks, countries like Australia, United Kingdom, and France have also showcased significant performances with total medals of 633, 550, and 449 respectively. Among the top contenders, there's a noticeable gender balance in medal acquisition, which reflects a level of gender parity in these nations' athletic prowess. On the other hand, nations like Canada and Japan have a relatively lower tally but still hold a respectable position in the global arena with total medals of 261 and 423 respectively.
